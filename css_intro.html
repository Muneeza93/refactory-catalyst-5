Education is a basic human right that powers development and is directly responsible for eradicating poverty, increasing life expectancy, ensuring political stability and gender equality.For the case of individuals, education boosts employment opportunities, entrepreneurship, household income and poverty reduction.

For Countries, education promotes sustainable economic growth through increase in revenue collection, strong government institutions free from corruption and spurs innovation. Developing countries are recording encouraging results of more children enrolling in schools.

However, a 2018 World Development Report shows that an increase in school enrollment doesn’t automatically translate to learning.The report indicates that 53% of all children in low- and middle-income countries cannot read and understand a short story by the time they are finishing primary school.

This is known as learning poverty.The World Bank defines learning poverty as the inability to read and understand at simple text by the age of 10.It is therefore a mandate of all stakeholders to take the necessary action to see to it that quality education is the number one priority.
